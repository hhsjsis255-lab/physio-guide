Low Back Pain (LBP)

Definition

Low Back Pain is pain or discomfort localized between the lower rib margin and gluteal folds, with or without radiation to the lower limbs.

Etiology / Causes

  • Mechanical: Muscle strain, ligament sprain, disc prolapse
  • Degenerative: Lumbar spondylosis, facet arthritis
  • Postural: Poor ergonomics, prolonged sitting
  • Others: Trauma, infection, tumor

Pathophysiology

  • Muscle strain → micro-tears → inflammation → pain
  • Disc prolapse → nerve compression → radicular pain
  • Degeneration → disc height loss → facet overload

Clinical Features

  • Localized low back pain
  • Pain aggravated by movement
  • Muscle spasm
  • Reduced ROM
  • Radiating pain (sciatica)
  • Numbness / tingling

Special Tests

  • SLR: Pain below knee (30–70°) → disc involvement
  • Slump Test: Neural tension
  • Kemp’s Test: Facet joint pain

🔥 Physiotherapy Clinical Diagnosis

Step 1: Subjective (history, pain type, aggravating factors)

Step 2: Observation (posture, gait)

Step 3: Palpation (tenderness, spasm)

Step 4: ROM (pain + restriction)

Step 5: Special Tests (SLR, Slump)

Step 6: Neurological Exam (dermatomes, myotomes, reflexes)

Step 7: Functional Assessment (ADL difficulty)

Clinical Impression:

  • Mechanical LBP → Local pain
  • Disc → Radiating pain + SLR positive
  • Facet → Pain on extension

🧠 Pattern Recognition

  • Localized pain → Mechanical
  • Radiating below knee → Disc involvement
  • Pain on extension → Facet issue
  • Neurological deficit → Nerve compression

Medical Diagnosis

  • X-ray → Degeneration
  • MRI → Disc herniation (Gold standard)
  • CT scan → Bony pathology

Physiotherapy diagnosis is clinical; imaging confirms.

Red Flags 🚨

  • Bladder/bowel dysfunction → Cauda Equina
  • Severe trauma
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Night pain

When to Refer

  • Neurological deficit
  • Fracture suspicion
  • No improvement (4–6 weeks)
  • Infection / tumor suspicion

Management

Mechanical: Mobilization, strengthening

Disc: McKenzie exercises, avoid flexion initially

Chronic: Postural correction, functional rehab

Clinical Flow

Presentation Diagnosis
Pain + lifting history Muscle strain
Radiating pain + SLR positive Disc prolapse
Extension pain Facet issue
Neurological deficit Nerve compression
Bladder/bowel symptoms 🚨 Emergency Referral

Clinical Pearl 💡

Radiating pain below knee + positive SLR = strong indicator of disc involvement.
