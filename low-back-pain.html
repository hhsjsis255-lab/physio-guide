Physio Study Guide

Low Back Pain Assessment

Patient Profile

Age, occupation, lifestyle, activity level

Subjective Assessment (History Taking)

  • Chief complaint
  • Onset and duration of pain
  • Pain type (sharp/dull/radiating)
  • Aggravating and relieving factors
  • History of trauma
  • Past medical history
  • Red flag symptoms

Objective Assessment

  • Observation (posture, gait)
  • Palpation (tenderness, muscle spasm)
  • Range of Motion (Active & Passive)
  • Muscle strength testing
  • Neurological examination
  • Special tests (e.g., Straight Leg Raise)

Step-wise Clinical Assessment (Physiotherapy Approach)

  1. Introduction & Consent: Explain procedure and take consent.
  2. History Taking: Ask about symptoms, pain behavior, and functional limitations.
  3. Screen Red Flags: Trauma, fever, weight loss, neurological signs.
  4. Observation: Assess posture, spinal curvature, and gait.
  5. Palpation: Identify tenderness, tightness, or spasms.
  6. ROM Assessment: Check lumbar movements (flexion, extension, side bending).
  7. Neurological Exam: Reflexes, sensation, muscle strength.
  8. Special Tests: Perform SLR or other relevant tests.
  9. Clinical Reasoning: Interpret findings logically.
  10. Physiotherapy Diagnosis: Mechanical LBP / Disc / Muscle issue.

Clinical Reasoning

If pain radiates to leg → nerve involvement
If localized pain → muscle strain
If pain increases on flexion → disc involvement
If pain increases on extension → facet joint issue

Differential Diagnosis

  • Disc prolapse
  • Muscle strain
  • Facet joint dysfunction

Red Flags 🚨

  • Severe trauma
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever or infection
  • Loss of bladder/bowel control
